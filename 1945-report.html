1945-56

Report On The Reorganisation Of Central Government (Tottenham)

As the nature and structure of the government changed in the years 1857, 1909, 1919, and 1935, the administration in India was given an overhand. However, an evolutionary perspective on the secretariat and civil service reforms since 1857 indicates British intentions of developing an administrative system in India that should be an amalgam of traditional, colonial, and liberal administrations.

All these reforms committees and commissions were constituted by the British not to concretize the administrative problems about which they had little knowledge but mainly because the reforms needed the approval of the Secretary of State for India, who was a responsible minister answerable to the British Parliament.
