600-300 BCE

The Age of Mahajanpadas

The age of the Janapadas began with the emergence of the 2nd urbanization in India (Janapada/MahaJanapada) in North India. Many of the Janapadas of the early period developed into MahaJanapadas by the 6th century B.C.E. The MahaJanapadas were mostly monarchical kingdoms where dynastic kings with absolute powers and a regular army ruled over a defined territory called Janapada. However, some of them were republics known as Ganasanghas/Ganarajya also existed . While there was a concentration of monarchies on the Gangetic plain, the republics were scattered in the foothills of the Himalayas and northwestern India. Some popular Mahajanapadas were Anga, Magadha, Kasi, Kosala4, Vajji, Malla, Chedi, Vatsa, Kuru, Panchala, Matsya, Surasena, Asmaka, Avanti, Gandhara and Kambhoja.

Lichachavis, mallas, shakyas, vajji etc. were some of the important republics. Some of the republics consisted of either a single tribe, and some were a confederacy of tribes.

The republican Sakya clan, instead of one absolute ruler, had many Kshatriya chiefs called Rajas. In the republic state of Vajji administrative and political affairs were debated in the Santhagara (assembly hall). The judicial administration of the Vajji state is also mentioned in the Pali texts. We also find the mention of Senapati as head of the army in the republic. The Mahabharata cites a republic known as Utsava-Sanketas as a great town-ruler on the banks of the Indus. The other republic described in the Mahabharata is the Sudras and the Abhiras, residing in the Indus Valley.
