The Trends Of Administrative Reforms From 1857 To 1947 Indicate That English Rulers Were Interested In:

(a) Developing a merit-based civil service where they were experimenting
(b) Building an efficiency-oriented administrative system and specific rational rules and regulations was a prerequisite.

  • The colonial goals were not compatible with liberalism and the welfare of the natives.
  • People’s participation in administration was neither possible nor desirable
  • Hence, the administrative system was deliberately kept elitist and rule-oriented

1. The Tottenham committee reported on the organization of departments, the question of staffing, and the reorganization of the entire secretariat system

2. It recommended that distinct duties be assigned to different grades of secretarial officers and agreed on nomenclatures for each stage.
