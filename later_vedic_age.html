1000- 600 B.C.

Later Vedic Age

The later Vedic period witnessed the tribes’ transformation into small settled communities transforming into kingdoms with capital and basic administrative arrangement. The shift from pastoral to a mixed farming economy affected the Later Vedic polity. The Tribal and clan identity of the Early Vedic eventually transformed into territorial identity, altering the status of the chiefs. The Rajan now was the protector of the territory, not just the protector of cattle. The powerful lineage of the Rigvedic period, Rajanya, now became the ‘Kshatriya’ i,e. having power over a territory. The Kshatriya received a presentation from the Vis in exchange for their protection and consequently became subordinated to the Kshatriya. A basic taxation system seems to appear in this period where Sangrihitri, treasurer of taxes, and Bhagadugha as tax collectors were important officials. Bali and Bhaga also emerged as regular tributes and taxes.
