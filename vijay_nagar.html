1336–1646 CE

Vijaynagar Empire

The Vijayanagar empire was founded in 1336 by the Sangama dynasty’s brothers Harihara I and Bukka Raya I, members of a cowherd group with Yadava ancestry. By the end of the 13th century, the empire had risen to prominence due to various Southern dynasties’ attempts to repel Islamic incursions. It conquered practically all of South India’s reigning dynasties. It pushed the Deccan Sultans beyond the Tungabhadra-Krishna River and annexed modern-day Odisha (ancient Kalinga) from the Gajapati Kingdom, establishing itself as a significant force. It lasted until 1646, albeit its authority waned after a severe military loss at the Battle of Talikota in 1565 by the Deccan Sultanates’ united army. The empire was named after the capital city of Vijayanagar. For reconstructing the administrative history of the Vijayanagar state, there are literary works available in Kannada and Telugu, whereas the inscriptions are in Kannada, Telugu and Tamil languages Kingship was hereditary but there has been a recorded instance of public involvement in selecting the king- at least to the extent that an unpopular candidate could not be appointed the king to avoid triggering social unrest. It has impressive ruins surrounding modern hampi, which is a world heritage site in modern karnataka, India. Kingship Krishnadeva Raya, explains the position of Kingship in his book ‘Amuktamalyda” that a King should gather round him people skilled in state craft and seek their advice and help in administration. “A crowned king should always rule with an eye towards Dharma”. Krishna Deva Raya advises the king that “with great care and according to your power you should attend to the work of protecting the good and punishing the wicked without neglecting anything that you see or hear.”.
